# CFO Question - Live Demo Response

> *"How do we know the AI notification is actually reducing our penalty clause triggers, and not just telling customers about delays they would have found out about anyway?"*

**Response (spoken at the whiteboard):**

We test it with a randomized controlled experiment so we can separate correlation from impact. The control group keeps today's operating process with no AI-triggered outreach, while the treatment group gets the proactive notification workflow we just designed. The one number I would track is penalty clause triggers per 100 shipments, pulled from the accounts payable and contract claims systems by shipment ID so finance and operations are looking at the same source of truth. With roughly 12,000 shipments a year, a 90-day test gives us about 3,000 shipments, or roughly 1,500 per group, which is enough volume to compare both groups with statistical confidence. If the treatment group shows a materially lower penalty-trigger rate after 90 days, we can attribute that lift to the notification process. The honest limitation is that this measures the full operating workflow, not just the model, so customer success follow-through and message quality can affect the result too.
